# Taqwā (تقوى) - God-Consciousness

## Definition
Taqwā represents comprehensive God-consciousness manifesting as spiritual vigilance and moral awareness, leading to protective self-restraint and ethical conduct.

## Key Aspects
- Constant awareness of Divine presence
- Ethical vigilance in all actions
- Protection against moral harm
- Inner state reflected in conduct
- Balance of hope and fear

## Historical Development
Central Quranic concept, appearing over 250 times in various forms. Developed through prophetic teachings and elaborated by successive generations of scholars, particularly in Sufi traditions.

## Contemporary Relevance
Fundamental to modern Islamic ethical decision-making, personal development, and professional ethics. Guides environmental and social responsibility.

## Notable Quotes
> "The most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous [atqākum]" - Quran 49:13
> "Taqwa is here" - Prophet Muhammad (pointing to his heart) - Sahih Muslim

## Related Practices
- Regular self-examination (muḥāsaba)
- Conscious decision-making
- Ethical consumption
- Regular worship practices
- Community service
